Language Structure

Independent Roles

In English, "Doctor Engineer" means an engineer who became a doctor, whereas “Engineer Doctor” means a doctor who became an engineer. Both are valid sayings but they have different meanings. In Arabic adjectives come after their nouns and therefore “مهندس طبيب” means an engineer who became a doctor, whereas “مهندس طبيب” means a doctor who became an engineer. Both are valid sayings but they have different meanings.

 

Generalization-Specialization Roles

In English, adjectives come before the nouns they describe.

"Surgeon Doctor" means a doctor who is surgeon, whereas in Arabic, adjectives come after their nouns so "طبيب جراح" is a surgeon doctor while "جراح طبيب" is “doctor surgeon” which is nonsensical.
